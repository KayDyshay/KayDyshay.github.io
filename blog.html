I've Got the Juice.
By Kanesha Dyshay / 28 August / Personal, Development / 3 comm.

Repeitition is the path to mastery

I was just told that a website that I'm learning to code on a specific site I'm using isn't the best way to land a job as a front-end web developer. I'd heard that around 5% make it out into the developer world. When I heard this, I admit, I considered dropping the site and starting over from scratch with another. Yet, after all of the hard work I've put in and the work I've accomplished, I know I'm too far along (and extremely motivated) to cave in.

No matter what others say, don't fall for the first thing that someone says. Stand your ground.

No matter what others say, don't fall for the first thing that someone says. Stand your ground. The person who told me this has immeasurable experience compared to mine, a Senior Developer to my Junior. I don't like to easily give up. I heard the message, though, and I'm considering alternate ways to include more information into my development routine to learn more. Talk about rewriting your goals and plans becoming a habit! Just when I got on track with this goal, a monkey wrench just HAD to get thrown my way.

But I'm not the type of person to let others define who I am. More importantly, I don't think the message was intended to deviate me from my goal, but to help guide me in the right direction. The only way I can get myself pointed there is by writing it down, not typing. A computer science professor, Professor Leonard Tharpe, you can't understand what you're trying to say unless you write it down.

A Few Specific Goals

  • Finish earning front-end developer certificate on FreeCodeCamp - 27 December 2017
  • Gain clients while in college - now thru December 2017
  • Get a business card! (I know...I know!) - now thru October 2017
  • Revise resume & post for jobs online
  • Begin contributing to open-source and non-profit projects - January 2018

These goals are now written in my journal for safekeeping and safe measuring! Just like they tell you when you earn a degree, 'No one can take your knowledge away from you.' I'll be damned if I let all of that knowledge go to waste!

I say all of this to say, 'Repetition is the Path to Mastery.' Because I've put in so much time and repeated the behaviors of learning code in the way I have, interrupting that flow can hurt me in the long run. As it would in other aspects in life. If you find that you've struggled with staying on course when finding out what to do, comment below and tell me about that time. I hope you enjoyed the read! I look forward to sharing my successes with you soon!
